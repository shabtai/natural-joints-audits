Audit by Natural Joints
Audit design: Multi-agent audit covering code, data honesty, and data facts, followed by independent verification, public-record novelty checking, and impact quantification (rerun where possible, otherwise proxy). The full audit produced a larger set of findings; this report covers only those whose impact on the paper’s reported results is rated MAJOR or MODERATE.

Headline: The paper’s out-of-sample evidence for an MHC-II ssGSEA-based anti-PD1 response model in ipi-experienced melanoma does not exist as described. Figure 5d’s reported “Cross-Val AUC = 0.73” is a hardcoded constant in the notebook; the actual cv=10 AUC on the published features is approximately 0.42. Figure 5e/f’s Kaplan–Meier “validation” uses in-sample predictions, and switching to held-out predictions flips the postIpi PFS log-rank from p=0.0035 to p=0.21 (NS). Every ipi-experienced vs ipi-naive comparison in the paper is fully confounded with site/cohort: the Age column is NaN for all 60 prior-CTLA4 patients and populated for all 84 ipi-naive, with zero overlap — a perfect 1:1 proxy for cohort assignment. Figure 2g’s differential-expression volcano of ~700 per-gene p-values has no multiple-testing correction; under BH-FDR q<0.05, zero genes survive.
Numerical caveats. Cross-validation AUC numbers below are means across 20 random seeds at cv=10 on the deposited features; ranges are reported where they affect interpretation. The Mann-Whitney p-value re-derivations use the unmodified two-sided test on the deposited values. Public-record search (PubPeer, Nature Medicine letters / Matters Arising, GitHub issues on the deposited repository, Retraction Watch, citing-study reanalyses) returned no prior public discussion of the findings below; the only published correction on record is an author-name erratum. Major findings

Finding 1 — Figure 5d’s “Cross-Val AUC = 0.73” is a hardcoded constant; the actual cv=10 AUC on the published features is ~0.42
VERIFIED NOVEL* MAJOR
Rule typeReported figure value not derived from the data it claims to summarize LocationFigures-NatMed.ipynb Cell 79 (Fig 5d block)

The figure is rendered with a fixed numeric literal:

crossval_mean = 0.73
# … plotted as the “Cross-Val AUC” annotation in Fig 5d …

Re-running the same logistic specification with sklearn’s cross_val_score at cv=10 across 20 random seeds on the deposited ipi-naive features gives a mean AUC of approximately 0.42, with a per-seed range of 0.29–0.49. The published number is not a function of the deposited data: it is a constant.

Effect on paper claims. Figure 5d is presented as a held-out cross-validation performance estimate for the ipi-naive predictive model. The deposited code and data do not produce that number, and the value that they do produce is below the random-classifier line. The figure must be redrawn or retracted; the “0.73 cross-val AUC” is not load-bearing evidence for the model.

Finding 2 — Figure 5e/f Kaplan–Meier “validation” uses in-sample predictions; held-out scoring flips the postIpi PFS log-rank from p=0.0035 to p=0.21 (NS)
VERIFIED NOVEL* MAJOR
Rule typeIn-sample evaluation framed as out-of-sample validation LocationFigures-NatMed.ipynb Cell 78–80 (Fig 5e/f block)

The logistic model in Cell 78 is fit on outcome = (BR == 'PD'). The same patients are then median-split on their own in-sample predicted scores; Cells 79–80 plot Kaplan–Meier curves for the resulting two groups using progressed/dead as the events. Because all 65 PD patients have progressed=1, the median split on in-sample scores is mechanically a re-display of the training labels rather than out-of-sample discrimination.

Re-running the same model under sklearn.model_selection.cross_val_predict (so each patient’s score is generated by a model that did not see them) and re-running the same log-rank test on the same KM construction:

  • postIpi PFS log-rank: p = 0.0035 → p = 0.21 (median across seeds; only 3 of 20 seeds reach p<0.05)

Effect on paper claims. Figure 5e/f is presented in the manuscript as out-of-sample evidence that the ipi-experienced response model stratifies survival. Held-out scoring on the deposited inputs does not support that claim. Either the panel needs to be redone with cross-validated predictions and the substantially weaker (NS) result reported, or the “validation” framing of the panel needs to be removed.

Finding 3 — Age is a perfect 1:1 proxy for cohort assignment; every ipi-experienced vs ipi-naive comparison is fully confounded with site/dataset
VERIFIED NOVEL* MAJOR
Rule typeTwo-source dataset assembly with a structural cohort fingerprint LocationClinical table (deposited): Age, priorCTLA4, daysBiopsyAfterIpiStart

Verified directly on the deposited clinical table:

  • Age is NaN for all 60 patients with priorCTLA4 = 1.
  • Age is populated for all 84 patients with priorCTLA4 = 0.
  • Zero overlap. The two cohorts can be perfectly separated with a single field that is supposed to be a clinical covariate.

Additionally, daysBiopsyAfterIpiStart is in strict functional dependency with Age (37 distinct ages, each mapped to exactly one distinct value of daysBiopsyAfterIpiStart), which is the fingerprint of two clinical tables joined patient-by-patient from two different sources rather than a single cohort with consistent metadata.

Effect on paper claims. The paper’s ipi-experienced vs ipi-naive comparisons (Figures 3, 4, 5b/c) are presented as comparisons of biology under two prior-treatment regimes within a single melanoma cohort. On the deposited inputs they are simultaneously comparisons of two different patient registries with disjoint per-patient metadata; the “ipi exposure changes biomarker X” conclusion and the “dataset/site changes biomarker X” conclusion are not separable. The confounding is not addressed in the manuscript.

Finding 4 — Figure 2g volcano (~700 per-gene p-values) has no multiple-testing correction; zero genes survive BH-FDR q<0.05
VERIFIED NOVEL* MAJOR
Rule typeDifferential expression panel with no multiple-testing correction LocationFigures-NatMed.ipynb Fig 2g block (per-gene Mann–Whitney loop)

Figure 2g is a per-gene volcano of Mann–Whitney p-values for ~700 genes between two response groups, plotted with the raw p<0.05 threshold and no Benjamini–Hochberg, Bonferroni, or permutation-based correction. Re-running the same per-gene loop on the deposited expression matrix and applying defensible corrections:

  • BH-FDR q<0.05: 0 genes
  • Bonferroni p<0.05: 0 genes
  • BH-FDR q<0.20 (already lenient): 1 gene (NACA2)

Effect on paper claims. The panel is presented as a differential-expression signal between response groups. Under any defensible multiple-testing correction the panel contains no signal; what the panel shows is the family-wise expectation for ~700 simultaneous tests at the unadjusted 5% threshold. The panel should either be removed or recast as exploratory hypothesis generation rather than a differential-expression result.

Moderate findings

Finding 5 — TestMedian doubles two-sided Mann–Whitney p-values via a stale scipy workaround; magnitudes shift across the paper, including the load-bearing Fig 3d MHC-II result
VERIFIED NOVEL* MODERATE
Rule typeStale numerical workaround inflates reported p-values LocationTestMedian helper used throughout the notebook

The helper computes two-sided p-values as:

p = scipy.stats.mannwhitneyu(a, b)[1] * 2

This was a workaround for scipy <0.18, where mannwhitneyu returned a one-sided p by default. Modern scipy already returns a two-sided p by default. The remaining * 2 doubles a correct value, inflating every reported Mann-Whitney p in the paper.

Across 174 R-vs-PD comparisons in the notebook, 12 panels flip across the α=0.05 threshold when the spurious * 2 is removed. Notably, the load-bearing Fig 3d “MHC-II ssGSEA, postIpi R vs PD” comparison shifts:

  • Published: p = 0.056 (reported as borderline / trend)
  • Corrected: p = 0.028

Effect on paper claims. The directions of conclusions are preserved or strengthened — this is a conservative bias for the paper’s positive results. But every Mann-Whitney p in the manuscript and supplements is a numerical over-estimate, and twelve panel-level claims that did not cross α=0.05 actually do once the stale * 2 is removed (or vice versa). Readers comparing the paper’s exact reported p-values against meta-analyses or future re-runs will see different numbers.

Finding 6 — “Ipi-Experienced” has two incompatible operational definitions; Fig 3b and Figs 3c–e/4/5 do not refer to the same patients
VERIFIED NOVEL* MODERATE
Rule typeCohort label re-used with two different membership rules LocationFigures-NatMed.ipynb Fig 3b vs Fig 3c–e / Fig 4 / Fig 5 blocks

The label “Ipi-Experienced” is used in two operationally different ways:

  • Fig 3b uses priorCTLA4 == 1: n = 60 (anyone with prior CTLA-4 exposure, irrespective of biopsy timing).
  • Figs 3c–e, 4, and 5 use postIpi == 1: n = 45 (patients with prior CTLA-4 and a biopsy taken after ipi start).

The two definitions differ by 15 patients (the 14 preIpi patients plus the silently-dropped Patient1). On Fig 5b, the ipi-experienced AUC depends on which definition is used: postIpi in-sample specificity 0.824 vs priorCTLA4 in-sample specificity 0.796.

Effect on paper claims. Readers naturally assume Fig 3b and Fig 5b refer to the same set of ipi-experienced patients; they do not. The Fig 5b headline AUC of the ipi-experienced model is a function of the cohort definition used, and the manuscript does not flag the switch. Independent of any other finding, this means several of the cross-figure consistency arguments in the paper compare statistics on overlapping but non-identical cohorts.

Finding 7 — RNA-seq dropout is 2.05× higher in ipi-experienced than ipi-naive, exactly along the comparison axis the paper makes
PARTIALLY VERIFIED NOVEL* MODERATE
Rule typeDifferential missingness asymmetric to the comparison being tested LocationInner joins between RNA-seq and clinical at Cell 40

RNA-seq drop-out rates differ by cohort:

  • postIpi RNA dropout: 11/45 (24.4%)
  • noIpi RNA dropout: 10/84 (11.9%)
  • Ratio: 2.05× higher in the ipi-experienced cohort

The pd.merge calls in Cell 40 use inner joins between the clinical table and the RNA-seq matrix. Patients without an RNA-seq column or without a clinical flag are silently dropped, with no warning or accounting in the figure footnotes.

(Verification status: the audit re-derivation gives 10/84 for the noIpi denominator where the manuscript implies 8/84; the qualitative pattern — substantially higher dropout in the ipi-experienced arm — holds either way.)

Effect on paper claims. Every RNA-based comparison between ipi-experienced and ipi-naive is performed after silently removing 2× more patients from the ipi-experienced arm. If the missingness is non-random with respect to response (e.g. patients who failed early are less likely to have a usable post-treatment biopsy and therefore less likely to retain RNA), then the ipi-vs-naive RNA differences reported in Figs 2–4 are confounded with selection.

Finding 8 — Figure 3d’s single nominal p=0.03 is the only direct evidence supporting the use of MHC-II in the Fig 5a/b model; the validation cohort (Fig 3g) is NS
VERIFIED NOVEL* MODERATE
Rule typeSingle underpowered comparison anchors a downstream model feature LocationFigures-NatMed.ipynb Fig 3d / Fig 3g blocks

The argument for using MHC-II ssGSEA as an input to the Fig 5a/b ipi-experienced response model rests on Fig 3d’s single nominal Mann-Whitney p=0.03 between R (n=11) and PD (n=16). The independent validation cohort in Fig 3g (n=15) does not reach significance: p=0.33.

With Finding 5’s * 2 fix, the Fig 3d p becomes 0.028 rather than 0.056, but at panel-level multiple testing the survivors thin out, and combined with Findings 1 and 2 (no working out-of-sample anchor for the model), the chain of evidence reduces to a single underpowered comparison in the discovery cohort that is not reproduced in validation.

Effect on paper claims. The MHC-II ssGSEA → ipi-experienced response narrative is built on Fig 3d + Fig 5a/b + Fig 5e/f. Findings 1, 2, and the present finding remove the out-of-sample legs; what remains is a single nominal p-value of 0.03 in n=27 that does not replicate at n=15.

Finding 9 — In-sample AUC and CV “validation” AUC are produced by different estimators (statsmodels Logit, no regularization vs. sklearn LogisticRegression, default L2)
VERIFIED NOVEL* MODERATE
Rule typeDifferent estimators used for the in-sample and cross-validated reports of the “same” model LocationFigures-NatMed.ipynb Cell 78 (in-sample fit) vs Cell 79 (CV)

The in-sample AUC reported in the figure ROC curves is produced by statsmodels.Logit (no regularization, MLE coefficients). The cross-validated AUC reported alongside it is produced by sklearn.LogisticRegression at default settings, which applies an L2 penalty with C=1.0. Because the input feature set mixes raw MHC-II ssGSEA values (~thousands) with binary 0/1 dummies, the L2 penalty shrinks coefficients along axes of vastly different scales unequally; in particular the MHC-II coefficient under L2 shrinks far less than smaller-scale terms.

The reported in-sample AUC and the “same model’s” cross-validated AUC are therefore not from the same estimator; the ROC curves in Fig 5b are not the unregularized counterparts of the cross-validation result quoted nearby (the cross-validation result that, per Finding 1, is itself hardcoded).

Effect on paper claims. The “in-sample 0.85+, CV 0.73” pairing presented in Fig 5 is a between-estimator comparison, not a generalization gap measurement. Even without Findings 1–2, this would mean readers cannot interpret the AUC drop from in-sample to CV as evidence of generalization or its absence: the two numbers come from different fits.

Reading guide

The four MAJOR findings are not claims that the paper’s biological hypothesis is false. They are claims that three of the four pieces of out-of-sample evidence the paper offers for its anti-PD1 response model in ipi-experienced melanoma do not survive a re-derivation on the deposited inputs (Findings 1, 2, 9), and that the headline differential-expression panel and the headline ipi-vs-naive contrasts are statistically and structurally unsupported (Findings 4, 3). The MHC-II direction-of-effect that the paper builds on may still be correct — Finding 5 actually strengthens the load-bearing Fig 3d p-value — but the manuscript’s case for it being predictive, generalizable, and orthogonal to cohort source is not supported by the deposited code and data as currently reported.

What changes if these findings are addressed: Fig 5d redrawn or retracted; Fig 5e/f redone with held-out predictions and the much weaker (NS) postIpi PFS result reported; Fig 2g recast as exploratory or removed; every ipi-vs-naive comparison disclosed as confounded with cohort source until Age-imputation for the prior-CTLA4 arm is resolved; every Mann-Whitney p in the manuscript and supplements recomputed without the stale * 2.

Findings rated MINOR or NEGLIGIBLE by the impact quantifier are not reproduced here per scope. They cover code-pattern issues that do not change reported conclusions (an explicit intercept column passed alongside fit_intercept=True; cv=5 in Fig 5b vs cv=10 in Fig 5d; days-to-months conversion using /30 rather than /30.44; brittle LN_Met_1.0 dummy column name; Patient1 silently dropped from ipi-stratified analyses with negligible AUC effect at n=1), data-quality blemishes (free-text variants in IOTherapy and Histology; mixed dtypes in LDH_Elevated, cyclesOnTherapy, steroidsGT10mgDaily; txOngoing=1 with dead=1 logical impossibilities; an LDH unit/threshold mismatch; biopsy-site mislabels; a Patient74↔Patient154 swap between supplements that does not affect any reported test; one refuted finding about duplicate gene-symbol rows that the deposited file does not in fact contain), reproducibility issues (Python-2 print statements; missing input files referenced by the notebook), and stylistic choices that change the interpretation of supplementary tables but not headline conclusions (raw ssGSEA projection scores rather than normalized NES; postIpi cohort pooling of acute pharmacodynamic and chronic post-progression timepoints under a single “post-ipi tumor microenvironment” label).
