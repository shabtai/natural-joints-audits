Liao 2020 BALF COVID-19 — Liao et al., Nature Medicine (2020)

Domain: Single-cell scRNA-seq of bronchoalveolar lavage in COVID-19 Generated: 2026-04-30

Single-cell atlas of 65,813 BAL cells from 13 donors (4 healthy, 3 mild, 6 severe COVID-19), used to argue for hyperinflammatory macrophage states and depleted tissue-resident populations in severe disease. Audit finds four claim-invalidating issues: cell-level DE and composition tests treat 65,813 cells as independent of 13 patients (~10106 p-value inflation vs patient-level), HC4 is an externally sourced epithelial-rich sample silently merged into the healthy arm, the severe arm's cellular evidence is 78% from two donors, and the mild arm is effectively n=2 because M3 contributes only 363 cells.

LORIS — Chang et al., Nature Cancer (2024)

Domain: Immune-checkpoint blockade response prediction Generated: 2026-04-29

A logistic-regression score (LLR6) for predicting ICB response, validated on Chowell, MSK1, MSK2, Kato, Ravi, Vanguri, and MSK_nonICB cohorts. Audit finds three structural generalization claims unsupported on the deposited inputs (MSK2 cancer-type vector mathematically inert, NSCLC train/test concatenation, Kato/Ravi degenerate Albumin/NLR), plus three moderate issues affecting AUC precision and external-validation framing.

PRECISE — npj Precision Oncology (2025)

Domain: Melanoma immune-checkpoint inhibitor response (single-cell) Generated: 2026-04-06

PRECISE (PREdicting Clinical Immunotherapy reSponse with single-cell Expression data) reports an AUC of 0.84 for an 11-gene XGBoost classifier trained with Boruta feature selection, RL label refinement, and leave-one-out cross-validation. Audit finds six compounding methodological problems — none previously reported — that together call the AUC into question and suggest the model may be learning to detect treatment effects rather than predict response.

6 methodological flaws

PRAIM — Eisemann et al., Nature Medicine (2024)

Domain: Breast cancer screening / AI decision-support Generated: 2026-04-17

A 461,818-study evaluation of AI-assisted mammography screening at 12 German sites. Audit finds a critical resampling-overwrite bug (1,000 iterations all use the original full dataset, CIs ~12% too narrow), a major omitted-confounder issue (screening_unit excluded from all propensity-score models despite Cramer’s V = 0.53 with treatment), and a counterfactual-claim issue (“cancers saved by AI” is a co-occurrence indicator, not a causal estimate).

DeepCDR — Cancer Drug Response Prediction

Domain: Drug response prediction (GDSC + cell-line expression) Generated: 2026-04

Data-integrity audit of DeepCDR, a deep-learning cancer drug response predictor still actively benchmarked in 2025–2026 publications. Two data bugs surface in the deposited GDSC inputs: (1) cisplatin is mapped to the wrong PubChem CID (an isomer rather than the active molecule), and (2) a threshold-mapping bug in the IC50 → sensitivity-label step. Both propagate into every downstream evaluation that uses the deposited drug list.
