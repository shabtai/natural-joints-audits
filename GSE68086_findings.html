GSE68086 — Tumor-Educated Platelets

Data-quality audit of the raw deposit behind Best et al., “RNA-Seq of Tumor-Educated Platelets…” (Cancer Cell, 2015) — the canonical blood-platelet liquid-biopsy benchmark, heavily reused yet not previously audited at the raw-data level.
Verdict: the dataset is largely clean. Every candidate below was checked against the raw matrix. No high-impact data bug survived verification — the findings are minor data-hygiene items and expected-by-design properties. Two items that first surfaced as “high-impact” were disproved on inspection (see “Did not survive verification”).

① Data-hygiene findings (real, low-impact)

Concrete, verified defects — easy to fix, low downstream consequence.

cell_type case split: Thrombocytes vs thrombocytes Confirmed3 detectors + raw data

One biological concept is stored under two spellings — 245× “Thrombocytes” and 40× “thrombocytes” — a case-sensitivity entry error that fragments the category and silently splits any groupby. Confirmed directly in the data (the 40 lowercase rows fall in one submission batch).

Evidence
witnesses GSM1817093, GSM1817094 = “thrombocytes”. Caught independently by the trap heuristic (case_whitespace_split), the cross-table invariant, and wrong-values; verified against the raw column.
Fix: df['cell_type'].str.capitalize().

Possibly inconsistent sample header: HD-3-1 vs HD-31 Ambiguousvocab check

Both HD-3-1 and HD-31 appear as matrix column headers. If they denote the same healthy donor written two ways, that silently breaks any join on sample id; if they are two distinct donors, it’s benign. Unresolved from the data alone — flagged for the depositor to confirm, not asserted as a defect.

Evidence
both headers present in TEP_COUNT_MATRIX; HD-3 absent, HD-1 present — consistent with either reading.

② Advisory — real but expected / by-design

Accurately-recorded properties that constrain analysis; not data errors.

Severe zero-inflation / limit-of-detection censoring Expectedtrap hunter

85.2% of values per sample are exactly 0, and 29,195 / 57,736 genes (50.5%) are zero across all 285 samples — characteristic of low-input platelet RNA, where zeros mean “below detection.” Methods assuming continuous expression will be biased.

Fix: pre-filter all-zero genes; use zero-inflated / negative-binomial models.

Per-sample mean counts differ ~3× Check normalizationreviewer

If the matrix is normalized, per-sample means should be comparable — yet Control-10 mean 48.4 vs Breast-100 17.8, HD-4 16.9 (reviewer found 2 scale clusters). Worth confirming whether columns are raw vs normalized before cross-sample comparison.

Batch imbalance & batch⟂cancer-type entanglement Advisorywrong-values

Samples are skewed across runs (Batch01 = 2 vs Batch03 > 100), and cancer types are not cleanly randomized across batches (Batch01 GBM-only; Batch05/06 Breast/Lung-skewed). Real and by-design, but the confound to control for before attributing the published classifier signal to biology.

Rare mutational subclasses (n=1) Advisorywrong-values

Subtypes such as MET and KRAS, MET appear once each — genuine biology, too sparse for per-subtype claims.

One within-patient replicate Minorraw-data check

Corrected from an earlier overstatement. A first pass framed this as systemic pseudoreplication; the raw data shows exactly one replicate pair in 285 samples — Lung-20 and Lung-20-1. Worth grouping by patient in any cross-validation split, but it is a single case, not a structural trap.

③ Correctly not flagged (precision)

Patterns a naïve checker false-alarms on; the enricher reframed them as legitimate conventions.

Sample names vs GEO accessions Convention

Count-matrix columns use investigator-assigned source_name values rather than GEO accessions — a standard GEO submission convention, not a mismatch.

EGFR↔Lung, KRAS↔Pancreas associations Expected biology

The correlation between cancer_type and mutational_subclass reflects real disease subtyping, not a derived/duplicated column.

④ Did not survive verification

Candidates the automated pass surfaced that the raw data disproved — kept here for transparency.

Value 999 = missing-data sentinel → false positive Disproved

An open-ended verifier asserted (at confidence 1.0) that a 999 in Vumc-NSCLC-57 was a sentinel for filtered data. The raw matrix says otherwise: 999 appears 67 times across 16.45M cells (once in that column) — the frequency of an ordinary count, not a systematic sentinel (counts range 0–289,755). Not a defect. The pipeline behavior that produced this has since been fixed (the wide path is now grounded in deterministic detectors, not open-ended LLM discovery).

Systemic pseudoreplication → overstated Downgraded

Initially framed as “rows are samples but keyed by patient → all 285 pseudoreplicated.” Verification found exactly one true replicate pair (see §2). Reframed to a minor note.

Methodology. Findings produced by the semantic-enricher pipeline (profiler → resolution → reviewer → trap hunter → grounded wrong-values verifier) over the raw GEO deposit, then each one verified against the deposited matrix. Severity reflects downstream impact, not detector confidence. The two §4 items are recorded to show the verification discipline — confident automated claims are checked, not trusted.
